## 3. Findings

### MAJOR

**M-1 — D-7 `replace dev.helix.agent` is dual-owned by SP4 and SP5 with no reconciliation.**
SP4 T4.0.5 (`SP4-plan:114`, "BLOCKS everything") and SP5 T5.2 (`SP5-SP6-plan:134-138`) BOTH add `replace dev.helix.agent => ../submodules/helix_agent` to `helix_code/go.mod`. The roadmap decision-register (b) (`roadmap:259`) assigns D-7 closure to SP5 ("first task adds the `replace` directives"), but SP4's plan claims it independently and neither plan cross-references the other. Under the "independent sub-programs" + ≥3 parallel streams (§11.4.103) delivery model, two streams editing the same go.mod `replace` block is a real edit-collision / duplicate-ownership defect.
*Fix:* designate ONE owner (roadmap leans SP5) for the `replace dev.helix.agent` edit; the other SP treats it as a precondition (no-op-if-present, assert via `go list -m dev.helix.agent`). Note SP5 additionally adds `replace dev.helix.substrate` — keep those distinct.
